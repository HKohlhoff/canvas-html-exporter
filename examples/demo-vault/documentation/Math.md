A formula can be written as inline math denoted by a pair of single \$-signs, e.g. $\nu = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}$ for the frequency. 
```md 
inline-mode
$\nu = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}$
```

In block-mode you write a pair of two \$-signs, e.g. for a simple integral on the real axis: 
$$
\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \sin(x^3)\, dx = 0
$$
^a1a814

The result follows directly without any calculation due to the odd symmetry on the function $\sin(x^3)$ and the interval being symmetric around the origin.

```md 
block-mode
$$
\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} \sin(x^3)\, dx = 0
$$
```
